Postfix expressions

The postfix expressions group from left-to-right and they are typically used to form function calls, subscripting and for structure and union member selection: 


postfix-expr:


primary-expr


postfix-expr [ expr ]


postfix-expr ( [arg-expr-list] )


postfix-expr . id


postfix-expr -5#5 id


postfix-expr ++


postfix-expr -

()
funcname() Function call.
[]
The array operator. See section § 3.12.8, pg: . The value in the bracket is used as an index into the array.
.
The struct or union address operator, see § 3.12.9, pg: .
-5#5
The struct or union indirect selection operator. see § 3.12.9, pg: .
++
The postfix increment operator, y = x++, will assign x to y and
then increment x by one. x must be an lvalue.

-

The postfix deincrement operator, y = x--, will assign x to y and
then deincrement x by one. x must be an lvalue.
